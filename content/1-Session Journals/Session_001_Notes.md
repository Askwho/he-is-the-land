- **Daylight spell:** Uses 2014 wording — produces **light, not sunlight**. No effect on sunlight-vulnerable creatures. 
- **Remove Curse:** Strictly **weaker in Barovia**. Will still do something but won't remove some things players expect — possibly not worth preparing.

- **Ammo is tracked** this campaign.
- NPCs matter and will lie/withhold; DM will foreshadow fairly and not "trick" the players.

## Cold open — converging on the stairs

Each PC, on a warm summer evening, out on a trivial errand:
- **Fëanor** (Waterdeep) — finding a blacksmith for the family horses; drew the short straw.
- **Lily** (Daggerford) — offering to mend boots for coppers.
- **Osric** (Goldenfields) — taking a message to the blacksmith re: making him armor.
- **Drew** (Julkem) — going to fix an old lady's clock.
- **Sirius** (Waterdeep) — off to his wine bar.

Each is overtaken by an unnatural **mist + temperature drop**; their **heirloom activates** (stone rolls away, feather floats off, banner streams out, tooth/amulet pull like a magnet) and leads them to the **bottom of a flight of stairs that does not belong** in their town (good Perception checks confirm the stairs are alien). A glow waits at the top.

They converge at the stair-foot in dense, walling mist. Drew's instruments (spinning compass, off temperature/pressure readings) confirm **this is not where any of them were**, and the conditions are wrong for Waterdeep.

**Time anomaly (key clue):** Sirius & Fëanor tried to walk back into the mist to "return to Waterdeep" — experienced **hours** of walking; for those who stayed at the stairs it was **seconds**. The mist is an impassable wall around the grounds. *Players correctly intuited a time/dimensional distortion.*

They ascend through an open **wrought-iron gate** onto spacious grounds: a four-story narrow house and a **cherub garden statue**. The front door **swings open by itself** at Sirius's knock.

## Death House — explored this session

### Entrance hall / foyer
- Warm, lantern-lit. **Shield on the north wall: golden windmill on a red field** — a genuine, battle-functional shield (Drew, Investigation 19). Sirius eventually straps it on.
- Once all five are inside: **door slams, fire dies, candles flare**, and a verse appears on the wall **in dripping red (blood)**:

> *Beneath this dwelling lurks a beast / Who hungers for a bloody feast. / He sleeps until the midnight chime / Then wakes to feed his dark design. / If morsels seek to flee their doom, / Then bring toward his secret room / A gift to soothe his savage mood / But mind the servants of his brood.*

- Grandfather clock strikes **6:00 PM (six bongs)** → effectively a **~6-hour countdown to midnight**, tracked in **real time**. Party correctly read the threat: find a gift for the beast → its secret room → or be eaten; beware the "brood/servants."

### Ground floor
- **Den (hunter's den):** Stag head + 3 **taxidermy** wolves (Sirius confirmed by hacking one — sawdust). Fur chairs, oak table, **cask of wine + 2 wooden goblets** (Sirius drank to test for poison — it's **turned to vinegar**, rotten not poisoned). **Longsword over the fireplace** — real, sharp, steel (not silver), **windmill carving on the hilt**. Locked **cabinet** (Drew failed to lever it; Lily forced it, Athletics 15) → **heavy, light & hand crossbows + 60 bolts (10 silvered, 50 normal).** Party clocked that the house is *equipping* them.
- **Dining room:** Opulent table, crystal & silverware, alpine-dale painting. Carved wall paneling of deer/trees → on a Perception 23, **twisted faces and lurking wolves** hidden in the carving. **Everything decorative is illusory/decayed:** silverware tarnishes to dust, crystal crumbles, food/wine is rotten, the painting disintegrates when removed (Dorian Gray effect). Solid furniture (chairs) is real.
- **Cloak room:** Six heavy cloaks, **adult and child sizes**. Drew piled them by the front door for the cold outside.
- **Kitchen + pantry:** Tidy; two sharp knives (usable daggers). Pantry food looks/smells fine but has an "off" tang — clearly unsafe to eat.
- **Suspected hidden space:** A gap/hollow-ish wall left of the dining room with no ground-floor door — party suspects access via another staircase. (This is the spiral-stair / nursery direction — not yet found.)
- Lily noted the firewood + tinder can be made into **portable torches**; party stockpiled equipment + torches by the front door.

### Second floor
- **Landing:** Marble hearth; **family portrait** — aristocratic man & woman, **two smiling children**, plus a **swaddled baby the mother regards with thinly veiled scorn**. Plaque/portrait mismatch noted. Four **suits of decorative armor**, each with a **real spear**; the metal could be worked (~1 hr) into **2 sets of scale mail**. Four spears collected.
- **Conservatory / music hall:** Harpsichord; beneath it a **starving hound, collar reads "Lancelot."** Lily cast **Speak with Animals** — dog is trapped, hungry, days here; everything is "grey, no smell"; **saw a "big two-legs" it could see through** (transparent figure → ghost). **Lancelot joins the party** (Lily & Osric vow to protect it; fed a femur later). Fëanor played the harpsichord (Perf 13).
- **Study:** Mahogany desk, framed windmill picture, floor-to-ceiling bookshelves + rolling ladder. Desk items: oil lamp (lit), ink, quill, tinderbox, **letter kit + wooden seal bearing the Durst family insignia**. Drawer held an **iron key** (Lily took it).
  - **Books are generic** (history, warfare, alchemy) — no Durst family history.
  - Rounded **scrape marks** on the floor → hidden lever. The book **"The Opening of this Door" by Paul Mybook** is the trigger; pulling it **swings the bookshelf open.**
  - **Secret room:** Tomes on fiend-summoning & necromancy of a cult, the **Priests of Osybus** — but Arcana (Drew 15, Fëanor 17) shows the books are **fakes / no real arcane value** (props).
  - End of room: **heavy iron-footed chest, lid ajar, a skeleton in leather armor jammed in it.** The **poison-dart trap was already sprung** (3 darts in the cuirass) — a prior victim died opening it. Fëanor used **Mage Hand** to open it & extract the skeleton. Inside: a **smaller chest** and a **letter**. Lily harvested the **leather armor** (offered to Fëanor).
  - **Smaller chest contents:** three blank books with black leather covers; three **spell scrolls — Bless, Protection from Poison, Spiritual Weapon**; the **deed to the house**; and a **signed will**. The will, signed by **Gustav and Elisabeth Durst**, bequeaths the house and all family property to **Rosavalda and Thornboldt Durst** on their parents' deaths. → Confirms the parents (Gustav & Elisabeth) and that the **two portrait children are Rosavalda & Thornboldt**; the resented baby (Frederick) is conspicuously **left out of the will**.
  - **The letter** (read in fragments): to *"My most pathetic servant,"* from a self-styled **dread lord**; *"Your husband took solace in the bosom of another woman, sire of monsters and dread propounder of madness"*; references **Barovia**; signed *"In darkness I endure."* Party inferred this is why the parents resent the baby (illegitimacy / monstrous parentage).
